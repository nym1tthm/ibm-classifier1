Rainwater Harvesting

Rainwater harvesting involves collecting and storing rainwater for future use. This technique helps in replenishing groundwater levels and reducing dependency on municipal water supplies.

Greywater Reuse

Greywater reuse involves recycling water from showers, sinks, and laundry for non-potable uses such as irrigation and toilet flushing.

Drip Irrigation

Drip irrigation is a water-efficient method that delivers water directly to the roots of plants, reducing water waste and promoting healthier plant growth.

Sanitation Measures

Clean Toilets

Ensure access to clean and hygienic toilet facilities to prevent contamination of water sources.

Sewage Treatment

Treat sewage water before releasing it into water bodies to avoid pollution.

Proper Hand Washing

Promote proper hand washing techniques to maintain personal hygiene and prevent the spread of diseases.

Clean Toilets

Providing access to clean and hygienic toilets is essential to prevent the contamination of water sources and to ensure the health and well-being of communities.

Sewage Treatment

Sewage treatment is the process of removing contaminants from wastewater, primarily from household sewage. It includes physical, chemical, and biological processes to remove pollutants and produce environmentally safe treated wastewater.

Proper Hand Washing

Proper hand washing is a critical hygiene practice to prevent the spread of diseases. It involves washing hands with soap and water for at least 20 seconds, ensuring all parts of the hands are cleaned.
